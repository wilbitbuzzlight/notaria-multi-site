Apostilles

International document authentication made simple.

What is an Apostille?

An apostille is an official certification that authenticates the origin of a public document so it can be used in countries that are members of the Hague Convention. It is the simplest and most widely accepted way to have your documents recognized internationally.

The apostille consists of a seal stamped (dry or electronic) by an authorized notary on the reverse side of the document or as an attached sheet. Only certified and accredited notaries are authorized to validate apostilles — our team is fully qualified.

Documents That Can Be Apostilled

  • Birth certificates
  • Marriage certificates
  • Death certificates
  • Academic diplomas and transcripts
  • Powers of attorney
  • Contracts and agreements
  • Court orders and judgments
  • Corporate documents
  • Wills and testaments
  • Divorce decrees
  • Bank statements
  • Invoices and commercial documents

Countries Where Apostilles Are Accepted

Apostilles are valid in all countries that are members of the Hague Convention, including:

  • Argentina, Bolivia, Chile, Colombia, Costa Rica
  • Ecuador, El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras
  • Mexico, Peru, Venezuela
  • United States and all other Hague Convention member countries
