Powers of Attorney

Authorize with confidence.

What is a Power of Attorney?

A power of attorney (POA) is a legal document that grants one person — the agent or attorney-in-fact — the authority to act on behalf of another person — the principal — in legal, financial, medical, or personal matters. It is one of the most important legal tools available, especially for individuals managing affairs across distances or dealing with health or travel situations.

A notarized power of attorney is legally required for it to be recognized by most institutions. Our notaries verify the identity of all parties and ensure the document is properly executed.

Types of Powers of Attorney We Handle

  • General Power of Attorney — broad authority over financial and legal matters
  • Special (Limited) Power of Attorney — authority limited to a specific task or transaction
  • Revocation of Power of Attorney — formally canceling a previously granted power

Common Uses

  • Authorizing someone to manage bank accounts or finances
  • Handling real estate transactions on your behalf
  • Signing legal documents when you cannot be present
  • Managing affairs for a family member abroad
  • Handling immigration or legal proceedings on another's behalf

How It Works

All parties involved must be present at our office with valid photo ID. The principal must appear in person to grant the power. Our notary will verify identities, witness the signing, and apply the official seal — making your document legally valid.

What to Bring

  • Valid government-issued photo ID for all parties
  • Completed power of attorney document (unsigned)
  • The principal must be present in person
