What is Contract Notarization?

Notarizing a contract means a licensed notary public verifies the identity of all signing parties and witnesses the execution of the agreement. This adds a critical layer of legal legitimacy — it helps prevent fraud, disputes over signatures, and can make a contract enforceable in court.

A notarized contract demonstrates that all parties entered the agreement voluntarily, with their identities verified and on record. Many landlords, lenders, businesses, and individuals require notarization for agreements to be considered legally binding.

Types of Contracts We Notarize

  • Lease and rental agreements
  • Service contracts
  • Purchase and sale agreements
  • Loan and promissory note agreements
  • Business partnership contracts
  • Independent contractor agreements
  • Any other legal agreement between parties

How It Works

All parties who need to sign must be present at our office at the same time. Bring your completed contract and valid photo IDs. Our notary will verify identities, witness signatures, and affix the official notary seal — usually completed within minutes.

What to Bring

  • Completed contract (do not sign before arriving)
  • Valid government-issued photo ID for all signing parties
  • All parties must be present at the same time
