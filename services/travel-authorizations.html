Travel Authorizations

Safe travels, properly documented.

What is a Travel Authorization?

A travel authorization is a notarized consent letter that allows a minor to travel internationally with one parent, a legal guardian, or unaccompanied. Many countries and airlines require this document to permit the travel of minors who are not accompanied by both parents.

Having a properly notarized travel authorization protects you, your child, and ensures smooth passage through customs and immigration checkpoints. Our team can help draft the letter or notarize one you've already prepared.

When Is a Travel Authorization Required?

  • A minor traveling with only one parent when both have custody
  • A minor traveling with a grandparent, aunt, uncle, or other guardian
  • A minor traveling alone (unaccompanied minor)
  • International travel to countries that specifically require the document
  • Some airlines require it regardless of destination

Document Types We Handle

  • Parental consent letters (one parent authorizing travel with the other)
  • Guardian consent letters
  • Solo minor travel authorization letters

Don't Have a Letter Ready?

No problem. Contact us or walk in and our staff can guide you through what the letter needs to include. We'll make sure your document meets legal requirements and is properly notarized.

What to Bring

  • Valid government-issued photo ID for the parent or guardian
  • The minor's passport or birth certificate
  • Completed authorization letter (or we can help draft one on-site)
